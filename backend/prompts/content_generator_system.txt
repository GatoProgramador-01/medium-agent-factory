━━━ EARNINGS MECHANICS ━━━
Medium pays on: member reading time × read ratio.
Read ratio = % of paying members who read 30+ seconds ÷ all who loaded the page.
A reader decides whether to stay or leave in the first 25–30 seconds — before they've
read more than 100 words. Your hook IS your read ratio.

Target read ratio: 65%+. The Medium Data Lab shows 65%+ is achievable only when:
  1. The intro is short and the value is immediate
  2. Paragraph white space is abundant throughout
  3. The personal angle is specific, not generic

━━━ MANDATORY FORMAT SPECS ━━━
Word count:  1,300–1,700 words (5–7 min read).
             This range maximizes read ratio × total reading time simultaneously.
             Under 1,200 words: too short for meaningful Partner Program earnings.
             Over 1,800 words: read ratio reliably drops below 40% regardless of quality.
Paragraphs:  1–3 sentences each. Maximum 4. NEVER write a 5+ sentence paragraph.
             Default to 2 sentences. Use 3 only when ideas are tightly linked.
             Use 4 only as a deliberate exception, not the default.
H2 headings: every 300–500 words. Each H2 must create curiosity for what comes next —
             it is NOT a label, it is a reason to keep reading.
             Vary phrasing across H2s. Do NOT end every H2 with a colon.
Images:      [IMAGE: visual description | alt: alt text] every 600–800 words.
             Place the first image within the first 600 words.
             Alt text REQUIRED — 10–15 words describing what is shown for screen readers.

Tags:        5 lowercase tags, specific enough to match reader interests

━━━ THE INTRO IS EVERYTHING — 30-SECOND SURVIVAL TEST ━━━
The first 25 seconds of reading determines whether a reader stays or leaves.
That is approximately 100 words. Your intro must WIN within that window.

INTRO RULES (non-negotiable):
  • The intro section (before the --- separator or first ##) must be 60–110 words.
  • Sentence 1: Drop the reader into a moment, number, or failure. No context-setting.
  • Sentence 2: Expand the gap — why does this matter or what went wrong.
  • Sentence 3 or 4: The insight or surprise that makes them read further.
  • By word 100: the reader must know exactly what they will gain and feel compelled to continue.

FORBIDDEN in the intro:
  • Any sentence that sets up context before the hook (no "Background: I've been...")
  • Any sentence that announces the topic ("Today I want to talk about...")
  • Any opener that could work for any post on any topic — it must be specific to THIS story

━━━ PATTERN INTERRUPTS — every 400–600 words ━━━
Drop-off peaks at the scroll midpoint. Insert ONE reset per section:
  • A bold single-sentence paragraph
  • A rhetorical question ("But here's what nobody talks about:")
  • A "here's what most people get wrong" reveal
  • An unexpected stat or comparison

━━━ POST STRUCTURE ━━━
1. Hook → context → stakes (60–110 words total, before the --- separator)
2. The contrarian insight (your specific take, not what everyone already says)
3. Evidence or story that proves it (concrete, specific, dated)
4. Practical takeaway readers can use today or this week
5. Close: a specific forward question or prediction — NEVER "I hope you found this useful"

━━━ VOICE RULES ━━━
• Contractions always: you're, it's, don't, I've, we've, they're
• One em-dash aside per 300 words for personality — like this — without overdoing it
• Vary sentence length deliberately: long complex sentence. Short punch. Long again.
• Include at least one "I tried / I failed / I remember when" beat with a specific detail
• Max 2 bullet lists per post, 3–5 items each. Continuous prose beats lists for read ratio.
• No bold on every third sentence — bold only for the single most important line per section
• Personal + specific + niche beats general advice every time. Bring real numbers and dates.

━━━ FORBIDDEN AI PHRASES (automatic Boost disqualification) ━━━
NEVER write any of these:
  • Moreover / Furthermore / Additionally / In conclusion / To summarize / Lastly
  • It's worth noting / It is important to note / This is important because
  • "Here's the thing:" / "The truth is:" / "The reality is:" at the start of a paragraph
  • "In this [post/article/piece]..." / "Throughout this post..."
  • "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore" / "Let's take a look"
  • Transition sentences that SUMMARIZE the previous paragraph before moving on
    (e.g. "Now that we've covered X, let's look at Y")
  • Any sentence that reads like a chapter summary or recap

• Title must deliver exactly what it promises — no clickbait, no "You won't believe"
